Set. Skip. Snooze.



Easy Alarms will allow you to quickly create and store alarms and timers on your iPhone or iPod touch. Unlike the Clock app, Easy Alarms allows you to skip repeating alarms and to save useful timers. You can even snooze a timer, just like an alarm. Easy Alarms will schedule notifications to be displayed on your phone whether or not you have Easy Alarms running.

Quick and Easy

Touch the plus ( + ) button for a new alarm or simply tap an existing alarm to go to its detail screen.

10-key time entry allows you to quickly set the time or duration in a few touches. For a 5 minute timer, just hit 5 and the Timer button. For a 7PM alarm, just hit 7 and the PM button.

Skip Upcoming Alarms

If you don't need your recurring alarm to go off today, but are worried you'll forget to turn it back on, just press SKIP and it won't go off this time. Don't need your daily alarm for several days? Just press SKIP until the next time its shows it will go off matches when you need it again.

Get Notified

When it's time for your alarm or timer, you will be notified with a system alert dialog even if Easy Alarms isn't running. The sound you chose will be played to get your attention. Note that Easy Alarms respects your mute setting, so you don't have to worry about alarms going off in the theater or at church.

Snoozability

Easy Alarms offers 2 ways to snooze your alarms and timers. When you pick a timed snooze, the alarm will fire a second time if you don't respond to the first alarm (Due to limitations of iOS, it will only snooze once automatically). When you choose 'Ask' you will be given the opportunity to specify a snooze duration when the alarm or timer goes off, allowing you to decide when to be notified again. You can keep an alarm or timer going for as long as you need to using Ask snoozing.

Set an Alarm for a Specific Day

With Easy Alarms, you can set an alarm to go off on a specific day. While you could use a calendar reminder to do this, an Easy Alarms alarm can be set up with a longer sound and an automatic snooze so you won't miss the alarm. Or you could choose to ask for a snooze so that you can put off the task for another hour.

Multiple Timers

Using Easy Alarms, you will be able to create multiple timers and have them all going at the same time or have them set up for later use. Perhaps you need a laundry timer that you always set for 30 minutes. Just create the timer and name it 'Laundry'. Later, flick the switch to turn it on when you're doing laundry. Need an extra 10 minutes to get the clothes really dry? No problem, just snooze the timer for 10 more minutes.
